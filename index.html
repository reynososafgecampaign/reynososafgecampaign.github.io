Hi, I'm Reynoso.
I'm running for President of AFGE 1680 at Terminal Island.
Our facility is in the midst of rapid change. During this time of uncertainty in our communinity, and nation politics, we as a community need to play a vital role in ensuring that our voices and values are heard and represented. I want to be an advocate for you and our neighbors.
Here's Why I'm Running
My parents came to the United States thirty years ago in search of a better life. They came to this country with humble beginnings, like many immigrants. As a result of their economic struggle, I learned how important it is to work hard, but I also gained a better understanding of how important it is to have strong institutions and a functional government. Despite the fact that I realize we have a long way to go in achieving justice and equality for all, our country's values motivate me.

Local politics may seem less important than national politics at first glance. However, these local topics affect our daily lives and play an important role in shaping our communities. Real political change is built from the ground up, as history has repeatedly demonstrated. That's why I'm running for the AFGE President.
Core Issues

Higher Pay


As president of AFGE 1680, I will continue to fight and make sure we get higher pay for our officers. We are actively pushing for automatic GS-9 for our officers. Our goal is for our correctional officers to recieve the appropriate salary due to thier work. We believe that those who have sacrificed much, deserve to be rewarded handsomely. I have made it a goal to continue fighting for pay that reflects experience and dedication to this field. As your AFGE 1680 President I will fight for higher pay for our officers.

Hazard Pay


During 2020 and 2021 many of our officers had to put their life on the line, as we faced an external threat, covid-19. Many of our officers had to come to work and risk getting infected. In a similar vein, many others were contaminated yet remained resilient throughout. I will make sure that those heroes don't go unnoticed. I will continue to fight for a 25% hazard pay for those who worked during the covid-19 pandemic.

Retention


Retention means keeping our best talent locally. I will continue to do this by fighting for a 10% retention, meaning all staff members will receive a 10% increase to their salary. Part of what makes our facility one of the best is diversity. However, not just any kind of diversity, diversity in terms of skillset. We each offer something new and interesting that help make our facility one of the nations best. I will continue to fight for appropriate compensation and competitive salaries.
What is AFGE 1680?
The American Federation of Government Employees (AFGE) is an American labor union representing over 670,000 employees of the federal government, about 5,000 employees of the District of Columbia, and a few hundred private sector employees, mostly in and around federal facilities. AFGE is the largest union for civilian, non-postal federal employees and the largest union for District of Columbia employees who report directly to the mayor (i.e., outside D.C. public schools).

If you work in the Terminal Island area, like I do, you are most likely part of AFGE 1680 region. AFGE 1680 has elections means to represent the constituents in the region. If you work at Termianl Island, I am running to represent you.
